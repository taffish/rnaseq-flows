# Code of Conduct

TAFFISH projects are built for careful, reproducible scientific work. We expect
discussion to be respectful, technically grounded, and welcoming to users with
different levels of biology, bioinformatics, software, and systems experience.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind, patient, and specific.
- Assume good intent while asking for evidence and reproducible details.
- Discuss ideas, data, code, and documentation rather than attacking people.
- Make space for newcomers and domain experts from different backgrounds.
- Avoid sharing private datasets, credentials, or personally identifiable
  information in public issues.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or conduct.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions after maintainers ask to refocus.
- Misrepresenting analysis results, provenance, or software behavior.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues; block accounts; or
take other moderation actions when needed to keep the project usable and safe.

If you see a problem, contact the maintainers through GitHub. For sensitive
security matters, follow `SECURITY.md`.
